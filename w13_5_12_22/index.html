We've touched on this previously but this let's you use multiple versions of Node in your terminal interchangeably. To install NVM, go to the following link: 

  • https://github.com/nvm-sh/nvm/blob/master/README.md#intro

React

By far the most popular frontend framework, in terms of both jobs and general use in the development community. Knowing React will expand your opportunities greatly and knowing it can help you jump to another pay grade. 

  • https://reactjs.org/docs/create-a-new-react-app.html

Svelte

The least used of the trio but consistently rates at the top in terms of developer experience. Although it is very new, people like to work in Svelte and will often choose it for things they build in their free time. With the framework being so new there isn't always the best support so you must be willing to get your hands dirty and build things yourself sometimes.
Although this is a major disadvantage, due to its popularity it is likely that Svelte will continue to mature and gain market share. In this case, you could be on the cutting edge of the newest technology which will make you primed for the roles that become available. Missed out on React and Vue? Dip your toe in Svelte and see what the future brings. 

  • https://github.com/sveltejs/svelte

Vue

Often seen as hate it or love it, Vue is the middle ground between React and Svelte in terms of usage. Companies are more likely to use Vue for lightweight applications or even auxillary applications to their main product. 

  • https://vuejs.org/guide/quick-start.html
